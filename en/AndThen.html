And Then There Were None

'And Then There Were None' is generally considered as one of the most acclaimed masterpieces by Agatha Christie. The play is based on the novel from 1939 by the same name. This book sold more than 100 million issues, making it the best-selling crime novel ever. We offer you an engrossing spectacle of exemplary quality, in a classical setting with the original, uneditted text.

The story

Although we want to spoil the plot for you by no means, we can reveal some small things about the setting of the story. Ten people who've never met before are (under various pretenses) invited for a short visist to an island. Soon it is revealed that none of them has ever seen their host or hostess in the flesh. A few strange occurences later, it becomes clear that there is a link to their presence on the island and an old 'nursery rhyme' about ten little indians....

Ten little Indian Boys went out to dine;
One choked his little self and then there were nine.

Nine little Indian Boys sat up very late;
One overslept himself and then there were eight.
